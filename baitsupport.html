Description

This course provides students with the knowledge and skills required to troubleshoot, maintain, and repair computer systems, networks, and software applications. Students will learn to identify and resolve common IT issues, configure and manage network devices, and provide excellent customer service as an IT support specialist.

Objective

  • Identify and troubleshoot common hardware and software issues
  • Configure and manage network devices and protocols
  • Install, configure, and manage operating systems and software applications
  • Provide excellent customer service and support
  • Understand IT security best practices and risk management
  • Develop problem-solving and critical thinking skills


First Year

1st Semester

  • Introduction to Computer Systems
  • Networking Fundamentals
  • IT Support Principles
  • Computer Hardware and Troubleshooting

2nd Semester

  • Operating Systems (Windows, macOS, Linux)
  • Software Applications and Troubleshooting
  • Network Protocols and Security
  • Help Desk Management


Second Year

1st Semester

  • Advanced Networking (TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP)
  • Server Administration (Windows, Linux)
  • Database Management (MySQL, SQL)
  • IT Project Management

2nd Semester

  • Advanced Operating Systems (Virtualization, Cloud Computing)
  • Network Security and Threat Management
  • IT Service Management (ITIL)
  • Web Development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript)


Third Year

1st Semester

  • Advanced IT Support (Troubleshooting, Problem-Solving)
  • Cloud Computing (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
  • Cybersecurity (Threat Analysis, Incident Response)
  • IT Research Methods

2nd Semester

  • Capstone Project (IT Support)
  • Electives (Choose one: Data Analytics, Artificial Intelligence, Internet of Things)
